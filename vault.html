The Knowledge Vault

The nerve system of the firm — one sovereign brain that every person and every agent learns from, and feeds.

Majd Al Kaid · Managing Director · Chair, VCG Group AI Committee

This is the flagship. The master deck ended on a teaser for this; we open here. The single idea: the Vault is VCG's owned, compounding IP — the thing that makes everything else (the cycle, the agents, the products) get smarter over time.
Why This Matters Most

The model is rented.
The loop is owned.

Every firm now builds two kinds of capital: human capital — our people's judgment and relationships — and token capital — the AI capability we build and own. The Vault is where they compound.

  • Models will keep getting better and cheaper — and everyone gets the same ones. No edge there.
  • The edge is the proprietary learning loop on top: our knowledge, our traces, our judgment, encoded and compounding.
  • You can offload a task — never your learning. The Vault is how VCG never offloads its learning.

Framing inspired by Satya Nadella's “human capital × token capital” thesis, June 2026.

Carry-through from the master deck, now the centrepiece. The board takeaway: spending on models is renting; spending on the Vault is building an asset that appreciates. This reframes the whole investment conversation.
In One Line

One brain. Connected to everything.
Read by all. Fed by all.

A single, sovereign knowledge layer — wired into every system, API and MCP we run — that our people query for their everyday work and our agents are steered by. And it gets sharper every time it's used.

Keep this slide clean and slow. It's the elevator pitch. "Read by all, fed by all" is the two-way loop — humans and agents both consume and contribute. Everything after this explains how.
The Problem Today

Our knowledge is scattered — and it walks out the door.

  • Expertise lives in people's heads, scattered chats, and folders no one can find.
  • Systems don't talk — the same numbers re-keyed across CRM, MT5, BI and spreadsheets.
  • Policies and playbooks sit in PDFs nobody reads until something breaks.
  • When someone leaves, their judgment leaves with them.
This is the pain the board feels viscerally. Don't dwell — name it sharply, because the Vault is the direct answer to each line. The "walks out the door" point is the emotional anchor: institutional memory is currently un-owned.

Made Concrete

Four verbs to remember: Ask, Drop, Steer, Compound. These map exactly to Majd's requirements — everyday querying, file-drop analysis, automations on dedicated resources steered by the vault, and the compounding loop. This is the slide people will quote back.
Why It's the Centre

The steering ball.

One source of context keeps ~100 people — and every agent — pointed the same way. The same facts, the same policies, the same memory, on tap. Decisions stop depending on who you happened to ask.

"Steering ball" is Majd's phrase and it's perfect — the firm's living compass. The strategic point: alignment at scale. 100 people making decisions from one shared brain instead of 100 private versions of the truth.
How It Compounds

A hill-climbing machine.

  • Query — people and agents use the Vault for real work.
  • Trace — every interaction and correction is captured as signal.
  • Evaluate — private evals score it on outcomes that matter to VCG — not public benchmarks.
  • Improve — the knowledge sharpens, and later the model does too. Then it loops.
Unlike most assets, the loop compounds — every use makes the next one better
This is the engine. The non-obvious insight: the traces from real work are the training data nobody else has. That's why the advantage is hard to replicate — competitors can buy the same model but not our loop.
The Maturity Ladder

From a knowledge layer
to VCG's own model.

Now
A unified knowledge layer (RAG) over Obsidian + every system, via MCP. A local model for sensitive docs. ~80% of the value, fastest.
Next
Capture the traces. Private evals on the outcomes that matter to VCG.
Then
Fine-tune a VCG SLM — the “company veteran,” on our own hardware.
Later
Private reinforcement on real traces — the frontier step, optional.

We do not train a model on day one. The knowledge layer earns its keep first; the SLM is phase two.

The honesty slide. It protects credibility: anyone technical knows you don't start by training a model. RAG-first is the disciplined path, and the SLM becomes worthwhile precisely because the loop has been feeding it good traces.
The Sovereignty Test

Swap the model.
Keep the veteran.

The real test of control: can we replace the underlying model — Claude today, our own SLM tomorrow, whatever's best next year — without losing the expertise the firm has accumulated?

  • Our IP lives in the Vault + traces + evals — not in someone else's weights.
  • That's how VCG avoids being commoditised by the model layer.
This is the architectural principle that everything else serves. If we ever feel "locked in" to a model, we built it wrong. The knowledge and the loop must be portable across models — that's sovereignty, and it's the board's assurance that this spend isn't a bet on one vendor.

The Connective Tissue

One layer, connected to everything.

  • MCP turns each system — MT5, CRM, Snowflake, Power BI, PSPs, Obsidian — into something the Vault can read and reason over.
  • 100 users hit one permission-aware brain — not a tangle of point-to-point integrations.
  • Add a new system once; every user and agent gains it instantly.
The "systems that don't talk" problem — solved at the layer, not per app
This is the practical payoff of MCP from the master deck, now the backbone of the Vault. The leverage point: integration is done once, centrally, and inherited everywhere. That's what makes 100 users on one brain feasible.

The Substrate

Markdown is the shared memory.

  • A shared Obsidian vault is the human-facing surface — and the agents' writable memory. Same files, both audiences.
  • Humans curate; agents append what they learn. Version-controlled, auditable, ours.
  • Plain markdown means no lock-in — the substrate outlives any tool or model.
Why Obsidian/markdown and not a proprietary KB: it's the LLM-native format, human-readable, git-versionable, and portable. The compounding happens here — this is the physical place "fed by all" lives. It also reinforces sovereignty: our knowledge is in open files we own.
The Separation That Matters

The Vault steers.
It doesn't run everyone's jobs.

  • Users build automations on their own dedicated compute — those workloads stay off the brain.
  • Vault agents steer them: supplying context, policy, and guardrails.
  • The brain stays fast and protected; automation load scales independently.
Exactly your model: dedicated resources, steered by the Vault
This is Majd's explicit design and it's architecturally smart — it decouples the knowledge/steering plane from the execution plane. The brain's job is context and governance; the heavy automation runs elsewhere. It's also how we keep the 100-user backbone responsive.
The Infrastructure

Sovereign — but not naïve.

Azure-private
GPU serving in our own tenant (UK South, private endpoints) — real concurrency for ~100 users, highly available.
Entra ID
Role-aware access control for the single layer — we already run it. The hardest piece, handed to us.
Claude API
The heavy-reasoning tier for non-sensitive work — tiered Haiku → Sonnet → Opus.
Sovereign enclave
A small on-prem box (Mac Studio / DGX-class) for data that can't leave + SLM fine-tuning.

Why not one Mac Studio for everything? Concurrency, serving stack, and reliability. A desktop is a brilliant enclave — not the firm's 24/7 backbone.

This is the resolved infra decision (Hybrid A). Be ready for the Mac Studio question — the honest answer is the bottom line: Apple Silicon has no batched-serving stack (no vLLM-class throughput), no ECC/redundancy/remote management, and the ecosystem is NVIDIA/Linux. So we use it as the sovereign enclave, with Azure-private as the concurrency backbone and Claude for heavy non-sensitive reasoning. Model-sovereignty means we can swap any layer without losing the Vault.
Security & Access

Trust, engineered in.

  • Closed-box: the Vault reasons over VCG data + whitelisted sources — never the open web.
  • Role-aware by default: not everyone queries KYC, payroll or finance — Entra enforces it at the door.
  • Human-in-the-loop on writes; AI narrates and proposes — it never touches the numbers.
  • Fully audited: every query and action is logged. Sovereign by design.
Same governance posture as the rest of our AI, applied to the Vault. The role-segmentation point is the one a regulated board will probe hardest — emphasise that access control is built at the single MCP layer via Entra, not bolted on per system. This is the slide that makes "one brain for everything" safe.
